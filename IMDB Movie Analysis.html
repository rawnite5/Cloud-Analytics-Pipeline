  • The rating distribution doesnt have outliers and resembles a rough normal distribution. On examining the runtimeminutes distribution, I see that eliminating a few extreme outliers can bring the distribution close to the normal but taking a log can eliminate skewness from outliers.
  • Since there are a lots of data points compared to the distinct values available of runtime (n = 238), years (121), the scatter plot between the three look cluttered for a unique combination of average rating and the other 2 variables. Thus, we will not be able to judge if there is a non linear pattern. Additionally due to computational challenges we will go ahead with linear model instead.
  • A rating voted by many users deemed to possess a higher confidence that the rating is true or close to the calculated one compared to a movie rating which were rated by a few. So we may assume that all movies are closer to the mean (our prior belief) if there are a few number of votes and closer to the true value for higher number of votes. However, it seems difficult to incorporate number of votes in movie rating since the rating will be manipulated in such a case and converge towards the mean for many movies which were rated lower. In this case almost all movies would have converged to around 6.5. So we wont weigh the rating based on number of votes. weighted_rating = mean_rating(1 - w) + wcurrent_rating where w = (n - min(n))/(max(n)-min(n)) and n = number of votes

We see a positive correlation between predicted rating and movie length i.e. longer movies were used to rate higher in the early years possibly because there were only a fewer movies back then and longer movies might be considered more entertaining during the early years. However, the trend between seems to decline/reversed as we progressed forward in time. One possible reason could be that people rate movies now based on the quality of the content. In the current fast paced world, people find movies boring which are longer (> 120 mins). Atleast I do.

Longer movies released before the 21st century tend to have higher IMDB ratings than the shorter counterpart. However, the trend is reversed for the movies released in the 21st century, i.e. longer movies tend to have a lower rating than the shorter counterpart.¶
